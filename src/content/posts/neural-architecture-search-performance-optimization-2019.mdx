Neural Architecture Search automates network design by searching over a space of possible architectures. The performance challenge isn't the final model — it's the search process itself. Early NAS (Zoph & Le, 2017) required 48,000 GPU-hours to find a competitive architecture. By late 2019, weight-sharing methods like DARTS and Once-for-All reduced this to under 1 GPU-hour. Understanding the performance trade-offs between search strategies is essential for practical NAS.

<Callout type="warning" title="The Weight Sharing Approximation">
  Weight sharing (ENAS, DARTS, Once-for-All) assumes that a sub-network's performance within the shared supernet predicts its standalone performance. This correlation is imperfect — high-ranking architectures from shared evaluation sometimes underperform when trained independently. Always fine-tune the top candidates from scratch.
</Callout>

## Hardware-Aware NAS: Searching for Efficiency

Standard NAS optimizes only for accuracy. Hardware-aware NAS adds latency, energy, or model size as constraints:

The key insight: the optimal architecture depends on the target hardware. A model optimized for GPU (wide, parallel) differs from one optimized for mobile CPU (depthwise separable, narrow). NAS can find these hardware-specific optima automatically.

<Callout type="tip" title="Practical NAS in 2024">
  For most practitioners, NAS is no longer about running search from scratch. Instead: (1) use pre-searched architectures (EfficientNet, MobileNet families) as starting points, (2) apply scaling laws to adjust depth/width for your compute budget, (3) if you must search, use Once-for-All or AutoSlim with a pre-trained supernet. The days of 48,000 GPU-hour searches are over.
</Callout>

## Conclusion

NAS evolved from impractical (48,000 GPU-hours) to routine (0.5-4 GPU-hours) through weight sharing and gradient-based search. The key performance optimization is architectural: weight sharing avoids training each candidate from scratch, reducing cost by 1000-50,000×. For production deployment, hardware-aware NAS (MnasNet, ProxylessNAS) finds architectures that are Pareto-optimal for the target device — achieving higher accuracy at the same latency than manually designed networks. In practice, the pre-searched EfficientNet and MobileNet families cover most use cases without running NAS yourself.
